Why You Need a Water Conditioner

If you use tap water for your aquarium, you must use a water conditioner. Here's why:

Chlorine and chloramine in tap water will kill your fish. These chemicals are added by water treatment plants to make water safe for humans - but they're toxic to fish gills and kill beneficial bacteria in your filter.

What Water Conditioners Do:

  • Neutralize chlorine - Instantly binds chlorine into harmless compounds
  • Neutralize chloramine - Breaks the chlorine-ammonia bond (important: chlorine alone doesn't handle this)
  • Detoxify heavy metals - Binds copper, lead, zinc from old pipes
  • Protect slime coat - Some formulas add aloe or protective colloids
  • Detoxify ammonia/nitrite - Premium conditioners like Prime also handle these

Does Your City Use Chloramine?

Over 30% of US water systems now use chloramine instead of chlorine. Chloramine does NOT dissipate on its own - leaving water out overnight won't help. Check your city's water quality report or call your water utility to find out.

Water conditioner is used every time you:

  • Perform a water change
  • Set up a new tank
  • Top off evaporated water with tap water
  • Treat an ammonia spike

Seachem Prime is the gold standard in the aquarium hobby. It's what fish stores use, what Reddit's r/Aquariums recommends, and what most experienced fishkeepers keep on hand. There's a reason it's been the #1 seller for decades.

What Makes Prime Special:

  • Complete protection - Neutralizes chlorine, chloramine, AND detoxifies ammonia, nitrite, and nitrate
  • Incredible concentration - 1 mL treats 10 gallons (just 2 drops per gallon)
  • Emergency ammonia treatment - Can dose up to 5x normal during ammonia spikes
  • Safe for all aquarium inhabitants - Fish, invertebrates, plants, corals
  • Cost-effective - A 500mL bottle treats 5,000 gallons

Pros

  • + Best value per gallon treated
  • + Detoxifies ammonia/nitrite for 24-48 hours
  • + Highly concentrated (less plastic waste)
  • + Safe for all aquatic life
  • + Indefinite shelf life

Cons

  • - Strong sulfur smell (normal, not harmful)
  • - Can interfere with ammonia test readings
  • - Concentrated formula requires careful dosing

Dosing Guide for Seachem Prime:

  • Normal use: 1 mL (1 capful = 5 mL) per 10 gallons
  • Emergency ammonia: Up to 5 mL per 10 gallons
  • New tank setup: 2 mL per 10 gallons
  • Water changes: Treat the volume of new water added

API Stress Coat is unique because it contains aloe vera, which helps heal damaged fish skin and rebuild the protective slime coat. If you have fish recovering from illness, injuries, or the stress of transport, this is the conditioner to reach for.

When to Use Stress Coat:

  • Treating injuries - Fin damage, scrapes, ulcers
  • After disease treatment - Helps rebuild slime coat damaged by medications
  • Handling fish - Net burns, hand contact
  • Tank moves - Relocating aquariums

Pros

  • + Contains healing aloe vera
  • + Replaces protective slime coat
  • + Reduces fish stress
  • + Handles chlorine and chloramine
  • + Widely available at pet stores

Cons

How to Use Water Conditioner Properly

During Water Changes:

  1. Calculate your water change volume - Use our Tank Volume Calculator if unsure
  2. Add conditioner to new water - Treat the water BEFORE adding it to the tank, or add conditioner directly to the tank right before adding new water
  3. Dose for the amount of new water - Not the total tank volume (unless doing a full water change)
  4. Wait 1-2 minutes - Let the conditioner work before adding to tank

Quick Dosing Reference:

  • Seachem Prime: 2 drops per gallon, or 1 capful (5 mL) per 50 gallons
  • API Stress Coat: 1 mL per gallon
  • Fritz Complete: 1 mL per 10 gallons
  • Tetra AquaSafe: 5 mL per 10 gallons

Emergency Ammonia Treatment:

If you have an ammonia or nitrite spike (common during cycling or after fish death), products like Seachem Prime and Fritz Complete can temporarily detoxify these compounds:

  • Dose up to 5x the normal amount
  • Effect lasts 24-48 hours
  • Redose daily until water changes or bacteria catch up
  • This buys time but doesn't fix the root cause - do water changes!

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I really need a water conditioner for my aquarium?

Yes, if you use tap water. Municipal water contains chlorine and chloramine that kill fish. Water conditioners neutralize these chemicals instantly, making tap water safe. The only exception is if you use RO/DI water or well water that has been tested and confirmed safe.

What's the difference between chlorine and chloramine?

Chlorine is a simple disinfectant that dissipates naturally when water sits out for 24-48 hours. Chloramine is chlorine bonded to ammonia - it's more stable and does NOT dissipate on its own. Most US cities now use chloramine, so you MUST use a conditioner.

How much water conditioner should I use?

Follow the dosage on your specific product. Seachem Prime uses 1 mL per 10 gallons (2 drops per gallon), API Stress Coat uses 1 mL per gallon. It's generally safe to slightly overdose (up to 5x) in emergencies, but avoid chronic overdosing.

Can I use water conditioner with fish in the tank?

Yes. Most conditioners are fish-safe and designed for this exact scenario. Add the conditioner to the new water before or as you add it to the tank.

Seachem Prime vs API Stress Coat: Which is better?

Seachem Prime is better for value and versatility - it treats more water per dollar and also detoxifies ammonia/nitrite. API Stress Coat is better if you have fish with injuries or compromised slime coats. Many hobbyists keep both.

Does water conditioner expire?

Most water conditioners have a shelf life of 3-5 years if stored properly. Seachem Prime can last indefinitely according to the manufacturer. Signs of degradation include color changes, unusual smell, or reduced effectiveness.
