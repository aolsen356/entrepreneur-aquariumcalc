Why You Need a Test Kit

Water quality is invisible. Your tank can look crystal clear while deadly ammonia builds up. A test kit is the only way to know what's actually happening in your water.

You need a test kit for:

  • Cycling a new tank - Track ammonia and nitrite during the nitrogen cycle
  • Diagnosing fish illness - Poor water quality causes 80% of fish diseases (stable temperature is also critical)
  • Weekly maintenance - Confirm your water changes are working
  • After adding fish - Monitor the bioload increase (use our stocking guide)
  • Troubleshooting algae - High nitrates fuel algae growth

What About Test Strips?

Test strips are convenient but have significant accuracy issues. We generally don't recommend them as your primary testing method.

When Strips Are OK

  • + Quick spot checks
  • + Travel/vacation monitoring
  • + Teaching kids about testing
  • + Backup to liquid tests

When Strips Fail

  • - Cycling a new tank
  • - Diagnosing fish illness
  • - Monitoring reef parameters
  • - Any situation needing accuracy

Warning: Test strips can show ammonia at 0 when it's actually at dangerous levels. During cycling, always use liquid tests.

How to Use Liquid Test Kits Properly

1. Shake the bottles

Especially Nitrate Bottle #2 - shake vigorously for 30 seconds before each use. Settled reagent causes false low readings.

2. Use the right amount of water

Fill to the line exactly. Too much or too little water skews results.

3. Wait the full time

Most tests need 5 minutes. Nitrate needs a full 5 minutes after shaking. Reading early gives wrong results.

4. Read in natural light

Artificial lighting changes color perception. Use daylight or a daylight lamp.

5. Rinse tubes between tests

Contamination from previous tests affects readings. Rinse 3x with tank water.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do test kits last?

Liquid reagents typically last 3-4 years from manufacture if stored properly (away from light and extreme temperatures). Check expiration dates on bottles.

Why is my nitrate test always showing 0?

You're probably not shaking Bottle #2 enough. It must be shaken vigorously for 30 seconds, and the tube must be shaken for 60 seconds after adding it. The crystals settle and won't work without proper mixing.

Can I use freshwater tests on saltwater?

Ammonia and nitrite tests work for both. pH tests won't read high enough for saltwater. You'll need a saltwater-specific kit for accurate marine results.

Do I need to test GH and KH?

For most community fish, no. For shrimp, African cichlids, or planted tanks with CO2, yes. The API GH/KH kit is a good add-on if needed.
